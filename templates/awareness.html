That login page was not real. In a genuine attack, the credentials you typed would now be in an attacker's hands. Don't worry — this is a safe training exercise. Here's what to learn from it.

🚩 How to spot a phishing page

  • Check the URL carefully — attackers use look-alike domains.
  • Look for HTTPS and a valid certificate, but don't trust it alone.
  • Be suspicious of urgency ("verify now or lose access").
  • Hover over links before clicking to see the real destination.

✅ Stay safe

  • Never share your password or OTP with anyone.
  • Use a password manager and unique passwords.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  • Report suspicious emails to your IT/security team.
